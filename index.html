About Afganistan

Afghanistan is a landlocked country located at the crossroads of Central and South Asia, bordered by Pakistan to the east and south, Iran to the west, Turkmenistan, Uzbekistan, and Tajikistan to the north, and China to the northeast.
It covers an area of approximately 652,864 square kilometers, making it comparable in size to countries like France or the U.S. state of Texas. Afghanistan has an estimated population of around 40 million people, most of whom are young.
The country is known for its striking natural landscapes, including the Hindu Kush mountains, the blue lakes of Band-e Amir, the historic Bamyan Valley, and culturally rich cities such as Herat, Kabul, and Mazar-i-Sharif.
Afghan culture is deeply rooted in history and tradition, reflected in its poetry, music, hospitality, traditional clothing, and celebrations such as Nowruz. The official languages are Dari (Persian) and Pashto, along with several other regional languages like Uzbek and Turkmen.
The vast majority of Afghans are Muslim, mainly Sunni with a Shia minority, and religion plays a significant role in daily life and cultural practices.

About the Neighbors

Iran

Iran is located to the west of Afghanistan. Its capital is Tehran, and its official language is Persian (Farsi). Iran has a long history, rich culture, and strong economic and cultural ties with Afghanistan.

Pakistan

Pakistan lies to the east and south of Afghanistan. Its capital is Islamabad, and its main languages are Urdu and English. Pakistan shares a long border with Afghanistan and has close trade and people-to-people connections.

China

China is located to the northeast of Afghanistan. Its capital is Beijing, and it is one of the world’s largest and most powerful economies. China and Afghanistan are connected through the narrow Wakhan Corridor.

Turkmenistan

Turkmenistan is north of Afghanistan. Its capital is Ashgabat, and the country is rich in natural gas resources. It maintains peaceful relations and trade links with Afghanistan

These are considered among the most important for political, cultural, historical, or economic reasons:

  1. Kabul — The capital and political center of the country
  2. Herat — A major center of culture, art, and Islamic architecture
  3. Balkh (Mazar-i-Sharif) — One of the most ancient and historical regions
  4. Nangarhar (Jalalabad) — Important for trade and border location
  5. Badakhshan — Known for its mountains and the Wakhan Corridor
  6. Bamyan — Famous for its history and the ancient Buddha statues
  7. Kandahar — Historically and politically significant in the south

Balkh

Balkh — the oldest and most famous,Balkh is often called the “Mother of Cities.”It is one of the oldest cities in Central Asia and the world.
It was once a major center of Zoroastrianism, Buddhism, and later Islam.
Many scholars, poets, and leaders came from this region, including Jalaluddin Rumi (who is associated with this area).
Historically, Balkh was an important part of ancient Persian, Greek, and Islamic empires.

Herat

Herat — ancient cultural center,Herat is also a very old city with a history of thousands of years.
It was a key city in the Timurid Empire and became a center of art, literature, and architecture.
The famous Herat Mosque (Great Mosque of Herat) and many historical monuments show its ancient importance.
